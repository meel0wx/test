This page was designed to look like the real Microsoft sign-in page. Could you spot the difference? Here are the red flags you should have noticed:

🚩 Red Flags on This Page

  • 🔓
    Insecure / wrong URL — The address bar showed micros0ft-login.suspicious-domain.com with a "0" instead of an "o", and no HTTPS padlock. Microsoft always uses login.microsoftonline.com.
  • 📧
    Unsolicited email link — Real companies rarely send urgent "verify your account" emails. Always go directly to the website yourself.
  • 🏷️
    Slightly off branding — Phishing pages often have subtle logo differences, wrong fonts, or odd spacing. Always compare carefully.
  • ⚡
    Urgency tactics — Phishing messages often pressure you ("Your account will be suspended!"). Legitimate services won't rush you into clicking.
  • 🔗
    Hovering reveals the real link — Always hover over links in emails before clicking to see the actual destination URL.
💡 How to Protect Yourself
  • Always check the full URL before entering credentials.
  • Use a password manager — it won't autofill on fake sites.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on all accounts.
  • Never click links in unexpected emails — type the URL directly.
  • When in doubt, call IT or the company directly.
  • Report suspicious emails to your security team immediately.
